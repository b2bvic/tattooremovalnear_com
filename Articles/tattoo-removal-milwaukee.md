## Treatment Process and Session Structure

Initial consultations assess Fitzpatrick skin type, ink depth, and realistic expectations. Practitioners photograph tattoos using standardized lighting. **Wood's lamp** examination detects metallic pigments.

**Q-switched Nd:YAG lasers** emit 5-10 nanosecond pulses at 1064nm for black ink, 532nm for reds. Sessions last 5-25 minutes. Patients describe hot grease or rubber band sensations.

**Picosecond lasers** deliver 450-750 picosecond pulses creating photoacoustic shockwaves. Treated areas exhibit immediate frosting followed by petechiae within 2-4 hours.

Pain management includes topical **lidocaine 5%** cream 30-40 minutes pre-treatment. **Zimmer Cryo** cooling reduces discomfort 40-50%. **Milwaukee Dermatology** offers **Pro-Nox** nitrous oxide for large tattoos at $75-$125 additional.

Injectable lidocaine provides strongest numbing, requiring physician administration. Dermatologists provide injection anesthesia for pieces exceeding 8x8 inches, adding $100-$200.

Aftercare involves antibiotic ointment and dressings for 72 hours. Avoid sun for 4 weeks, no pools for 7-10 days.

## Session Frequency and Timeline

Milwaukee practitioners space treatments 8-12 weeks apart allowing lymphatic clearance. Black ink clears in 6-8 sessions over 12-18 months. Resistant colors require 10-14 treatments spanning 20-28 months.

The **Kirby-Desai Scale** predicts counts using skin type, location, colors, density, scarring, and layering. Professional tattoos respond predictably versus amateur work.

Progress plateaus occur when immune response slows—practitioners switch wavelengths or extend intervals to 12-16 weeks.

## Milwaukee Climate Considerations

Wisconsin's harsh winters (December-February average 15-30°F) affect treatment planning. Indoor heating creates dry air that dehydrates skin, hardening collagen matrices. Use humidifiers and apply moisturizers (**CeraVe**, **Cetaphil**) twice daily during winter.

Snow and ice increase fall risks for 7-10 days post-treatment when skin remains vulnerable. Schedule early in winter (November-December) before peak ice season.

Summer's moderate sun (UV index 7-9) still demands photoprotection 4 weeks post-treatment. Mineral sunscreens at SPF 50+ prevent hyperpigmentation.

**Great Lakes activities** (swimming, boating) require caution. Lake Michigan water temperature (55-70°F summer) and bacterial counts necessitate 2-week waiting period before swimming.

Spring and fall (March-May, September-November) offer optimal treatment windows with moderate temperatures (45-65°F) and manageable sun exposure.

## Comparing Technologies in Milwaukee

**Q-switched lasers** dominate budget clinics due to proven efficacy and costs under $120,000. Generate thermal shattering but more collateral damage. Ideal for simple black tattoos.

**Picosecond lasers** deliver 30-100x faster pulses fragmenting through pressure waves. Milwaukee clinics charge $50-$100 more per session, justified by 20-40% session reductions.

**Combination systems** like **Astanza Trinity** toggle between modes. **Removery** locations use Trinity exclusively.

**Fractional lasers** like **PicoWay Resolve** preserve surrounding skin. Limited availability—only **Milwaukee Dermatology** offers fractional at $325-$550/session.
