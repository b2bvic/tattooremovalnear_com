## Treatment Process and Session Structure

Initial consultations assess Fitzpatrick skin type (critical in Memphis's 64% African American population), ink depth, previous treatment history, and realistic expectations. Practitioners photograph tattoos using standardized lighting. **Wood's lamp** examination detects metallic pigments resistant to conventional wavelengths.

**Q-switched Nd:YAG lasers** emit 5-10 nanosecond pulses at 1064nm for black ink and 532nm for reds. Laser energy shatters ink particles into lymphatically clearable fragments. Patients describe sensations like hot grease or rubber band snaps—uncomfortable but tolerable. Sessions last 5-25 minutes.

**Picosecond lasers** like **PicoWay** and **PicoSure** deliver 450-750 picosecond pulses creating photoacoustic shockwaves fragmenting ink with minimal heat. This preserves tissue and proves more effective on stubborn colors. Treated areas exhibit immediate whitening (frosting) followed by petechiae within 2-4 hours.

Pain management varies. Most apply topical **lidocaine 5%** cream 30-40 minutes pre-treatment. **Zimmer Cryo** cooling devices reduce discomfort 40-50%. **Memphis Dermatology Clinic** and **Germantown Dermatology** offer **Pro-Nox** nitrous oxide for large tattoos at $75-$125 additional.

Injectable lidocaine provides strongest numbing for extensive work, requiring physician administration. **Memphis Dermatology Clinic's** dermatologists provide injection anesthesia for pieces exceeding 8x8 inches, adding $100-$200.

Aftercare involves antibiotic ointment (**Bacitracin** or **mupirocin**) and non-stick dressings for 72 hours. Clinics provide kits with **Aquaphor**, sterile gauze, cleanser, and instructions. Avoid sun for 4 weeks, no swimming pools for 7-10 days.

## Session Frequency and Total Timeline

Memphis practitioners space treatments 8-12 weeks apart allowing lymphatic clearance. Compressing below 6 weeks increases scarring without accelerating results—biological clearance rate determines pace.

Black ink clears in 6-8 sessions over 12-18 months. Resistant colors (yellow, fluorescent green) require 10-14 treatments spanning 20-28 months. The **Kirby-Desai Scale** predicts counts using six variables: skin type, location, colors, density, scarring, layering.

Professional tattoos respond predictably versus amateur work. Cover-ups layering colors demand 30-50% more sessions. Progress plateaus occur when immune response slows—practitioners switch wavelengths or extend intervals to 12-16 weeks.

Memphis's humid subtropical climate (average humidity 65-75%) affects healing. High summer humidity increases lymphatic retention, paradoxically slowing ink clearance. Schedule treatments during fall and spring (September-November, March-May) when humidity drops to 55-65%.

## Comparing Technologies Available in Memphis

**Q-switched lasers** (Spectra, Medlite C6) dominate budget clinics due to proven efficacy and lower costs (<$120,000). Generate thermal shattering but cause more collateral damage. Ideal for simple black tattoos on fair skin.

**Picosecond lasers** (PicoWay, PicoSure, Enlighten) deliver 30-100x faster pulses fragmenting through pressure waves. Memphis clinics charge $50-$100 more per session, justified by 20-40% session reductions. Effective on previously treated resistant tattoos.

**Combination systems** like **Astanza Trinity** toggle between modes within sessions. Handles multicolor efficiently—1064nm for black, 532nm for reds, 694nm for greens. **Removery** locations exclusively use Trinity.

**Fractional lasers** like **PicoWay Resolve** use microbeams preserving surrounding skin. Reduces scarring in overtreated areas. Limited availability—only **Memphis Dermatology Clinic** offers fractional at $325-$550/session.
