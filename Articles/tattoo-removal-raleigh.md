## Triangle Climate and Treatment Timing

### Piedmont North Carolina UV Profile

The Triangle sits in the North Carolina Piedmont at approximately 35.8°N latitude. UV exposure follows a moderate southeastern pattern:

- **Summer (June-August):** UV index 8-10
- **Fall (September-November):** UV index 3-6
- **Winter (December-February):** UV index 2-4
- **Spring (March-May):** UV index 5-8

The October-through-March window provides 6 months of manageable UV levels. The Triangle's four-season climate — real winters without extreme cold — creates a balanced treatment environment. Neither the persistent sun of Florida nor the perpetual gray of the Pacific Northwest.

**Optimal treatment timing:** Begin September-October. Complete 3-4 sessions during the low-UV fall and winter months. Spring sessions work with standard protection. Schedule summer sessions only on covered treatment areas or with commitment to rigorous sun management.

### Humidity and Healing

Triangle summers bring humidity (70-80%) and heat (90°F+) that affect aftercare. High humidity supports skin hydration during healing but promotes bacterial growth. Keep treated areas clean and dry during the first 48-72 hours post-treatment. The spring-to-fall pollen season (which the Triangle endures with particular intensity) is irrelevant to tattoo removal aftercare — pollen affects respiratory systems, not laser-treated skin.

## Technology Availability

**PicoSure** (**Cynosure**): Available at approximately 5-8 providers. Established presence in Raleigh and Durham practices. The 755nm wavelength targets green and blue inks.

**PicoWay** (**Candela**): Approximately 3-6 providers. The tri-wavelength platform (1064nm, 730nm, 532nm) provides comprehensive color coverage and safe treatment for all skin types.

**Enlighten III** (**Cutera**): Limited availability, approximately 1-3 providers. See [Enlighten III Laser Review](/articles/enlighten-iii-laser-review.html).

**Q-Switch Nd:YAG:** Available at most providers. Appropriate for simple black tattoos, budget-conscious patients, and a reliable option for darker skin types at the 1064nm wavelength.

## North Carolina Regulatory Framework

North Carolina regulates laser procedures through the **North Carolina Medical Board**. Key requirements:

- Laser tattoo removal is classified as the practice of medicine
- Non-physician operators may perform laser procedures under physician delegation with documented training and supervision
- The **NC Medical Board** requires that the supervising physician establish treatment protocols and maintain oversight
- North Carolina's regulatory framework places clear emphasis on physician responsibility for delegated laser procedures

**Verification:** The **North Carolina Medical Board** provides online license verification. Check your provider's active licensure, board certification, and disciplinary history. North Carolina maintains a publicly searchable database with detailed provider information.
