# PicoWay Resolve vs PicoWay Zoom: Handpiece Differences for Tattoo Removal

**PicoWay Resolve** and **PicoWay Zoom** represent specialized handpiece attachments for **Candela**'s **PicoWay** laser platform, each optimized for distinct clinical applications. **Zoom** handpieces deliver traditional full-beam picosecond pulses across 4mm, 6mm, and 8mm spot sizes, designed for standard tattoo removal through direct ink fragmentation. **Resolve** handpieces fractionate the laser beam into microbeam arrays creating controlled dermal injury patterns, primarily used for skin resurfacing, acne scar treatment, and melasma rather than direct tattoo ink targeting. While both attachments mount on **PicoWay** systems, **Zoom** performs 95% of tattoo removal procedures; **Resolve** addresses post-removal textural irregularities and specialized applications like stubborn pigment dispersed across large areas.

## Beam Delivery Mechanisms

Fundamental optical differences define each handpiece's clinical capabilities:

**Zoom Full-Beam Delivery**: **Zoom** handpieces transmit unfractioned picosecond pulses at 1064nm, 785nm, or 532nm wavelengths. Energy distributes uniformly across selected spot sizes (4mm, 6mm, 8mm circles), delivering fluences of 0.3-0.8 J/cm². Uniform energy distribution fragments concentrated ink deposits efficiently—the standard approach for removing defined tattoo boundaries and dense pigment areas.

**Resolve Fractional Delivery**: **Resolve** handpieces split the beam into 100 microbeams per square centimeter, each measuring 200-400 microns in diameter. This fractional pattern creates microscopic treatment zones surrounded by untreated skin bridges. Fractional delivery penetrates 200-400 microns deep, targeting upper dermis while preserving epidermal integrity. Energy density within microbeams reaches 2-4 J/cm² despite lower overall fluence—sufficient for collagen remodeling but inefficient for concentrated ink fragmentation.

**Physical Distinction**: **Zoom** handpieces resemble compact cylindrical barrels 3-4 inches long with circular apertures. **Resolve** handpieces appear larger (5-6 inches) with rectangular or square aperture patterns visible as grid arrays. Handpiece swap takes 15-30 seconds via quick-release connectors, allowing mid-session transitions when specialized applications arise.

## Primary Clinical Applications

Each handpiece serves distinct treatment goals:

**Zoom Tattoo Removal**: **Zoom** performs traditional laser tattoo removal across all ink colors accessible to **PicoWay** wavelengths. The full-beam delivery concentrates photoacoustic energy precisely on ink particles, shattering pigments into lymph-clearable fragments. **Zoom** handles 95% of routine removal cases—professional and amateur tattoos, single and multi-color designs, small symbols to large sleeves.

**Zoom Cover-Up Preparation**: Partial removal for cover-up artwork uses **Zoom** exclusively. Lightening tattoos to 50-60% original intensity requires targeted ink fragmentation impossible with fractional patterns. Cover-up preparation constitutes 25-30% of tattoo removal procedures, all utilizing **Zoom** handpieces.

**Resolve Skin Resurfacing**: **Resolve** addresses acne scars, surgical scars, wrinkles, and photodamage through fractional dermal remodeling. Microbeam-induced injury stimulates collagen synthesis and epidermal regeneration. This application operates independent of tattoo removal in most clinical practices.

**Resolve Pigment Disorders**: Melasma, post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation, and sun damage respond to **Resolve** fractional treatment. Dispersed pigment scattered across large facial or body areas requires different approaches than concentrated tattoo ink. **Resolve** treats these diffuse pigmentation patterns where full-beam **Zoom** would cause excessive thermal injury across broad areas.

**Resolve Post-Removal Texture Correction**: After tattoo clearance, residual textural irregularities (smoothness differences, slight depressions) may remain. **Resolve** fractional treatment stimulates collagen remodeling to normalize texture. This represents 5-8% of tattoo removal cases requiring supplemental resurfacing after successful ink clearance.

## Tattoo-Specific Use Cases

When does **Resolve** apply to tattoo removal scenarios?

**Traumatic Tattoos**: Blast injuries, road rash, or workplace accidents embed foreign material (asphalt, dirt, metal fragments) creating irregular pigmentation. These "tattoos" disperse pigment unevenly across broad areas rather than concentrated deposits. **Resolve** fractional patterns address diffuse contamination where **Zoom** full-beam would over-treat, though traumatic tattoo removal remains uncommon (less than 2% of cases).

**Extensive Coverup Preparation**: Removing large, faded tattoos spanning 100+ square inches (full back, full sleeves) sometimes benefits from **Resolve** fractional passes to lighten dispersed residual ink. However, most technicians still prefer **Zoom** for controlled ink-specific targeting even on large areas.

**Cosmetic Tattoo Removal**: Permanent makeup (eyebrows, eyeliner, lip liner) occasionally uses **Resolve** for final remnant clearance after **Zoom** removes bulk pigment. The fractional approach theoretically reduces risk around delicate facial features, though evidence supporting superiority over conservative **Zoom** parameters remains limited.

**Ghost Shadowing Treatment**: Faint residual outlines persisting after maximum **Zoom** sessions sometimes respond to **Resolve** fractional stimulation of deeper immune clearance. Success rates vary—10-15% improvement reported in 30-40% of treated shadowing cases. Many clinics skip **Resolve** entirely, accepting ghost shadowing as treatment endpoint.

## Treatment Protocol Differences

Procedural approaches vary significantly between handpieces:

**Zoom Session Structure**: Technician selects wavelength (1064nm for black, 532nm for red), spot size (4-8mm based on tattoo detail), and fluence (0.4-0.8 J/cm² based on skin type). Two to three passes cover the entire tattoo until uniform frosting appears. Treatment duration: 5-15 minutes for small-medium tattoos, 20-40 minutes for large pieces.

**Resolve Session Structure**: Fractional treatment uses lower fluences (0.3-0.5 J/cm²) distributed across microbeam arrays. Single pass typically sufficient per session. Treating tattoo-sized areas takes 10-25 minutes due to grid pattern requiring multiple handpiece passes to achieve full coverage. Less common in tattoo-focused practices—resurfacing protocols occupy separate treatment tracks.

**Pain Perception**: **Zoom** produces sharp snapping sensations rated 6-7/10. **Resolve** fractional delivery creates distributed prickling sensations rated 5-6/10—less intense but covering larger areas. Topical anesthetics (BLT cream) reduce both to 4-5/10. Injectable lidocaine rarely necessary for **Resolve** due to lower peak intensity.

**Healing Timelines**: **Zoom** causes focal purpura, edema, and occasional blistering resolving in 7-10 days. **Resolve** fractional injury produces pinpoint bleeding (petechiae), diffuse erythema, and mild edema clearing in 5-7 days. **Resolve** preserves epidermal bridges enabling faster re-epithelialization despite treating larger surface areas.

## Clearance Efficacy for Tattoo Ink

Direct removal performance differs dramatically:

**Zoom Clearance Rates**: Professional black ink tattoos achieve 85-90% clearance in 6-8 **Zoom** sessions. Multi-color designs clear 75-85% in 10-14 sessions using wavelength-specific **Zoom** passes. **Zoom** represents gold-standard full-beam picosecond performance for concentrated ink removal.

**Resolve Clearance Rates**: No formal studies quantify **Resolve** tattoo removal efficacy because the handpiece wasn't designed for this application. Anecdotal clinical reports suggest 10-20% ink reduction per **Resolve** session—dramatically inferior to **Zoom**. The fractional pattern leaves untreated skin bridges where ink remains untouched, requiring 3-5× more sessions for equivalent clearance.

**Why Resolve Underperforms on Tattoos**: Concentrated tattoo ink demands focused energy delivery. **Resolve** fractional patterns waste energy creating dermal injury in non-inked tissue while delivering insufficient fluence to inked zones for optimal fragmentation. The microbeam approach optimizes collagen stimulation, not ink shattering—fundamentally mismatched to tattoo physics.

**Appropriate Resolve Applications**: Post-tattoo-removal texture correction, treatment of faded old tattoos dispersed across large areas, or addressing residual pigmentation patterns. **Resolve** functions as supplemental tool rather than primary removal mechanism.
