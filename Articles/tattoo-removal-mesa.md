## Treatment Process and Pain Management

Initial consultations assess Fitzpatrick skin type, ink depth, and realistic expectations. Practitioners photograph tattoos using standardized lighting. **Wood's lamp** examination detects metallic pigments.

**Q-switched Nd:YAG lasers** emit 5-10 nanosecond pulses at 1064nm for black ink, 532nm for reds. Sessions last 5-25 minutes. Patients describe hot grease or rubber band sensations—uncomfortable but tolerable.

**Picosecond lasers** deliver 450-750 picosecond pulses creating photoacoustic shockwaves. Treated areas exhibit immediate frosting followed by petechiae within 2-4 hours.

Pain management includes topical **lidocaine 5%** cream 30-40 minutes pre-treatment. **Zimmer Cryo** cooling reduces discomfort 40-50%. **East Valley Dermatology** offers **Pro-Nox** nitrous oxide for large tattoos at $75-$125 additional.

Injectable lidocaine provides strongest numbing, requiring physician administration. Dermatologists provide injection anesthesia for pieces exceeding 8x8 inches, adding $100-$200.

Aftercare involves antibiotic ointment and dressings for 72 hours. Avoid sun for 4-6 weeks (critical in Arizona), no pools for 7-10 days.

Arizona's extreme heat and low humidity (10-20%) create unique challenges. Desert dryness dehydrates skin, hardening collagen matrices and reducing laser penetration. Use humidifiers indoors and apply moisturizers (**CeraVe**, **Cetaphil**) 3-4 times daily.

**UV radiation intensity** averages 10-11 on UV index year-round, among highest in U.S. Sun exposure within 4-6 weeks causes hyperpigmentation in 30-40% of patients. Mineral sunscreens at SPF 50+ essential, reapply every 90 minutes outdoors.

Summer temperatures exceeding 115°F (June-August) increase infection risk through perspiration. Schedule treatments during cooler months (November-March, average 65-75°F).

**Pool culture** in Mesa creates compliance challenges. Wait 2 weeks before swimming, 3 weeks for hot tubs.
