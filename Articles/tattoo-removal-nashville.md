## Treatment Process and Pain Management

Consultations assess Fitzpatrick skin type, ink depth, and expectations. **Wood's lamp** examination detects metallic pigments. **Q-switched lasers** emit 5-10 nanosecond pulses at 1064nm for black, 532nm for reds. **Picosecond lasers** deliver 450-750 picosecond pulses creating photoacoustic shockwaves.

Pain management includes topical **lidocaine 5%** cream and **Zimmer Cryo** cooling. **Nashville Skin & Laser** offers **Pro-Nox** nitrous oxide at $75-$125 additional. Injectable lidocaine available for large tattoos, adding $100-$200. Aftercare involves antibiotic ointment and dressings for 72 hours.

## Tennessee Regulations and Licensing

**Tennessee Code Annotated § 63-6-204** requires laser operators to work under physician supervision or hold APRN licenses with laser training. The **Tennessee Board of Medical Examiners** mandates 40 hours education and 20 supervised procedures. Facilities register devices with **Tennessee Department of Health** and maintain liability insurance.

## Session Frequency and Timeline

Nashville practitioners space treatments 8-12 weeks apart. Black ink clears in 6-8 sessions over 12-18 months. Resistant colors require 10-14 treatments spanning 20-28 months. The **Kirby-Desai Scale** predicts counts using skin type, location, colors, density, scarring, and layering.

## Nashville Climate Considerations

Tennessee's humid subtropical climate (average humidity 65-75%) affects healing. High summer humidity increases lymphatic retention, paradoxically slowing clearance. Schedule treatments during fall and spring (September-November, March-May) when humidity drops to 55-65%.

Summer UV radiation (UV index 8-10) demands strict photoprotection 4 weeks post-treatment. Mineral sunscreens at SPF 50+ prevent hyperpigmentation. Winter fluctuating temperatures (30-50°F) and indoor heating dehydrate skin—use humidifiers and moisturize twice daily.
